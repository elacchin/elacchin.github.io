Awards

My PhD thesis has been awarded the "Christine Vandemoortele for research" Prize by the Deparment of Physics and Astronomy of the University of Bologna.
My thesis is aimed at deepeing our understanding of the formation and evolution of multiple stellar populations in globular clusters, by means of sofisticated 3D numerical simulations. In particular, I studied the effects of various physical processes such as cluster rotation and Type Ia supernovae on the formation of the second, and chemically peculiar, population.

I have co-supervised two graduate students at the Indiana University (US). Their 3D visualizations illustrating complex features of a rotating globular clusters were awarded the Faculty Assistance in Data Science Showcase Grand Prize. Two of their visualizations are shown below; on the left, helium-rich stars, while on the right the helium-poor ones are displayed.

During my PhD, I was awarded the MARCOPOLO grant by the University of Bologna to spend three months at the Observatoire de Paris, where I conducted my research with Alessandra Mastobuono-Battisti. I exploited N-Body simulations to study the possibility for a massive globular cluster composed of two generations of stars to lose most of its mass, to recall the present-day features.

I was awarded the scholarship by the Collegio Universitario di Eccellenza “Luciano Fonda” to carry on my Master studies in Physics.
The award covered accomodation and universitary taxes for the whole cycle of study.
